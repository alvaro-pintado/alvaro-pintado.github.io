The Halting Problem

A Conceptual Proof

Posted on August 24, 2014

The Halting Problem is to find an effective procedure that, given any Turing machine \( M \), say represented by its Turing machine number \( m \), and given any number \( n \), will enableus to determine whether or not the machine, given that number as input, ever halts. (Boolos, 2007) We will sketch the proof of why the Halting Problem is not solvable by a Turing machine.

I will give what I call a 'conceptual proof' which is an un-rigorous argument meant to illustrate the main idea of the proof with the details hidden.
