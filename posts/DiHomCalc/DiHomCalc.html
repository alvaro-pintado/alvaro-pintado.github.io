Directed Homology of a Directed Graph

Automated homology calculations with Python

Posted on July 14, 2020

Simply, we can take the directed homology of a directed graph to be the number of directed cycles of the graph. This nicely reduces to finding a non-negative solution to a system of linear equations. For a more detailed explanation of directed homology, see my other post.

Issues with Current Implementation

There is not a unique form to the basis of the solutions to the system. Under one kind of form, the basis represents the directed cycles and under another form it does not. My current implementation will produce the desired basis most of the time, but I used a dirty hack. I did not find a good solution to this issue by the end of the research program. The current implementation is on my GitHub

If I were to continue working on this, I would try to implement a different algorithm than the linear programming methods I was using. It turns out that the solution space to the systems that appear in this context are convex cones, and the generating vectors represent the directed cycles. There are some algorithms to find the generating vectors of a convex cone. I would look to implement those.
