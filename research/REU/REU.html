OSU REU 2019

Directed algebraic topology is the generalization of algebraic topology to the setting of directed topological spaces—spaces equipped with a notion of directionality (intuitively understood as the flow of time). Studying irreversible paths on topological spaces has applications in modeling irreversible phenomena, most notably in concurrent programming. There are competing ideas regarding how homology should be defined in the directed setting. Under the guidance of Dr. Sanjeevi Krishnan and alongside a peer research partner, I worked on a theory of directed homology proposed by Dr. Krishnan. My project investigated Dr. Krishnan’s proposed definition.
